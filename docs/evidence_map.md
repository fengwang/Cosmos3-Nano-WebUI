# Evidence Map - Single-GPU Comfort and ADHD-Friendly Onboarding

Date: 2026-07-24

Rules:

- Claims without evidence are marked speculative.
- Speculative claims cannot become MUST-level requirements.
- Owner decisions can become contract constraints, but technical feasibility
  still needs verification by the owning session.
- This repository is public. Entries must not cite private hosts or bake in
  private absolute paths; environment variables are cited by name only.
- The three ADHD reports (`docs/adhd/`) are provider research reports (Gemini,
  ChatGPT, QWen). Where they disagree, the disagreement is stated and one path
  is chosen (rows E-11..E-16). Report recommendations that contradict
  repository reality or the GitHub-Markdown rendering model are recorded as
  **not adopted**, with the reason.
- Evidence IDs restart per phase; cross-phase facts are cited to their archived
  location.

## Speculative / owner-decision markers

- **Owner decisions (not evidence-derived), binding as contract constraints:**
  the 1800 s value (E-01 establishes *which* knob; 30 min is the owner's chosen
  value, PRD Decision 2); the punchy-hook + caveats-at-the-bottom placement
  (PRD Decision 7); single-README structure (PRD Decision 6). These are
  testable as written and so may carry MUST requirements about *form* (a
  default of 1800; caveats present and visible; one README), but the
  *experiential* benefit ("feels comfortable on a 5090") is **speculative** and
  is **not** a blocking gate.
- **Speculative, not promoted to MUST:** that 30 min (vs. some other value) is
  the optimal keep-warm window; that the specific ADHD techniques measurably
  improve comprehension for this audience (the reports cite general evidence,
  not evidence about this repo). These inform SHOULD/qualitative goals only.
